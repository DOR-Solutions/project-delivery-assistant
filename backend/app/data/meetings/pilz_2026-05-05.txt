3. Delivery Performance & Schedule Review
3.1 General Progress
• Mandatory Heathrow five-year electrical testing impacted transfers during the previous
week. Resources were redeployed to direct lines to maintain productivity.

Heathrow Public
• No anticipated stoppages for the coming week.
3.2 Schedule Status (Summary)
• Programme re-sequenced to prioritise SCA 24 and TRA 74, ahead of Areas 21, 26, 40,
and 7, to support earlier mitigation release.
• Area completion status:
o Area 25: ~95% complete; commissioning at ~80%; final bag test and go-live
remaining.
o Area 22: ~95% complete; commissioning underway (~33%).
o Area 20: ~70% overall.
o Area 23: ~40% mechanical installation.
o Areas 264 & 66: ~90–95% complete (minor electrical/mechanical outstanding).
o Area 265: ~10% mechanical installation (new start).
3.3 Go-Live Clarification
• Area 25 go-live confirmed for night of 12 May into the morning of Wednesday 13 May.
• “Go-live” defined as technical switch from Pilz to Siemens safety system.
• Operations clarified that this does not constitute BAU recovery; operational constraints
remain unchanged.
4. Communications & Operational Risk
• Strong concern raised regarding the potential misinterpretation of “go-live” messaging
by operational stakeholders.
• Agreement that communications must clearly state that operational requirements (e.g.
zone restrictions, timings) remain unchanged.
• Risk highlighted that positive messaging could raise unrealistic expectations before
wider system stabilisation (likely post-summer).
5. Transfers & Dependency Management
• Transfers progressing well despite short-term stand-downs last week.
• Explicit request reiterated:
o Areas 264 and 265 must not be taken out simultaneously, due to loss of the
PBA exit route.
• Historical outages cited where loss of multiple routes resulted in manual bag portering;
avoidance of repeat scenarios stressed.
6. Bag Handling Performance
• Approximately 8,370 bags handled during reporting period.
• Thursday recorded the highest volumes.

Heathrow Public
• Minor outages on Lines 222 and 220 resulted in short-duration die-back; recovery
achieved within ~10 minutes.
• Temporary bag portering (~301 bags) used to protect collector lines.
• BA Ops request to clear signage/storage from small room near E1 has been fully
actioned.
7. HBS & Screening Performance
• Belt realignment on Line 222 caused extended outage and journey times up to ~9.5
minutes.
• No missed bags attributed to this delay.
• Data indicates system operating close to capacity; outages propagate rapidly to
check-in.
• Stakeholders supported re-introducing structured review of performance data in weekly
meetings.
• Request made to explicitly include Level 2 / Level 3 screening capacity within
reporting, due to its impact on die-back risk.
8. Forward Look & Key Activities
8.1 Reclaim 11
• Planned start date: 8 June 2026.
• BA staffing numbers and costs largely agreed for Reclaim 11.
• Confirmed this is a trial / prototype run, limited to Belt 11; future belts to be addressed
separately.
8.2 Trials – 21 May
• Concerns raised around readiness, RTGs, failure modes, and trial start times (03:00 vs
later).
• Engagement with Ops and Karis required to align operational constraints with trial
objectives.
8.3 SCADA Deployment
• RTG raised for SCADA deployment covering 6 additional areas.
• Purpose is to ensure adequate wrap-around support during deployment activities.
8.4 Screening Contractor (ICTS)
• Agreement that ICTS must be fully engaged ahead of trials.
• Any deviation from standard screening operations to be captured and logged as learning
for future phases.
